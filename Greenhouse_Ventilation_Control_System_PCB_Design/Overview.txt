The PCB was designed as a 2-layer board following standard industry practices to ensure electrical reliability, safety, and ease of manufacturing.

The top layer (F.Cu) is used for signal and power routing. Trace widths were selected based on the electrical role of each net:

Signal traces (including 3.3 V): 0.30 mm

Other low-current control and logic traces: 0.25–0.30 mm

Mains Traces : 1 mm

CLearance : 3.2mm

Higher-current paths (fan, relay, and load connections): wider traces were used accordingly to safely handle the required current

The bottom layer (B.Cu) is implemented as a solid ground plane (GND copper pour) covering the full PCB area. This provides a low-impedance ground reference, improves noise performance, and simplifies routing. Ground stitching vias were added at key locations to ensure all top-layer ground points are properly connected to the bottom ground plane.

Component placement was optimized to maintain clear separation between high-voltage and low-voltage sections, minimize trace lengths, and improve overall layout robustness.

Mounting holes were placed according to the enclosure dimensions to ensure proper mechanical alignment.

A complete Design Rules Check (DRC) was performed after routing and copper pours. The final design reports 0 errors, 0 warnings, and 0 unconnected nets, confirming the PCB is electrically correct and ready for fabrication.

All manufacturing files (Gerbers, drill files) and the BOM have been generated.
